# Business Context

## Overview

This project simulates the operational challenges faced by a growing B2B SaaS company managing inbound sales activity across multiple acquisition channels.

As lead volume increases, manual tracking introduces several problems:
- inconsistent qualification
- delayed reporting
- poor visibility into conversion performance
- slow sales response times

Revenue Operations teams solve these issues by creating centralized systems that connect:
- marketing acquisition
- lead qualification
- sales pipeline activity
- reporting and analytics

This project recreates that workflow using lightweight cloud tools and automation platforms.

---

# Business Objectives

The primary business objectives were:
- improve visibility into lead quality
- standardize qualification criteria
- automate operational workflows
- monitor funnel conversion performance
- provide stakeholder-ready reporting

---

# Operational Challenges

## Manual Reporting

Without automation:
- pipeline updates are delayed
- reporting becomes inconsistent
- stakeholders lack real-time visibility

## Inconsistent Qualification

Different sales representatives may evaluate leads differently, reducing operational consistency.

## Limited Funnel Visibility

The business struggles to answer questions such as:
- Which acquisition channel converts best?
- Which regions generate high-quality leads?
- Where are leads dropping off in the funnel?
- Which leads should sales prioritize first?

---

# Proposed Solution

The solution combines:
- workflow automation
- lead scoring
- API enrichment
- centralized operational storage
- business intelligence dashboards

The system automates the lead lifecycle from initial submission through opportunity progression and reporting.

# Revenue Operations Goals

The project was designed to simulate key RevOps functions:

# Analytical Focus

The analytics layer focuses on:
- funnel conversion analysis
- acquisition source performance
